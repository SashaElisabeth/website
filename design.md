# Design Properties

## Aesthetic Direction

**Structured creativity** — an elegant, refined brand that holds space for expressive, colourful process
work. The visual identity pairs a high-contrast display serif with flowing script, signalling both
authority and warmth. Think botanical illustration meets art-therapy studio: precise and beautiful on
the outside, expressive and alive on the inside.

This is a Dutch vaktherapie (expressive/art therapy) and coaching practice. The aesthetic should feel
European, calm, and professional — not the American luxury-wellness world, but a grounded, creative
healing space.

- **Full name:** Sasha Elisabeth
- **Subtitle:** Vaktherapie en Coaching

- Name: bold, high-contrast display serif — thick/thin contrast (Bodoni, Didot, or similar transitional

- Subtitle: flowing italic script, lighter weight, sits below the name
- Background: warm parchment (`#f7efd2`) — the logo lives on this ground

## Typography

### Scale & Roles
| Role          | Style                                           | Notes |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------|-------|
| Logo / Brand  | Bold high-contrast display serif, ALL CAPS      | Bodoni Moda or Playfair Display Black — thick/thin stroke contrast |
| Script accent | Flowing italic script                           | Paired with the logo for "vaktherapie & coaching" |
| Display / H1  | Transitional serif, regular–medium weight       | Editorial scale, generous line-height |
| H2 / H3       | Same serif, lighter weight, possibly italic     | Section headlines |
| Eyebrow       | All-caps sans-serif, tracked wide, very small   | Labels above headlines |
| Body          | Refined sans or light serif, ~60–65ch wide      | 1.7–1.8 line-height |
| Caption/Meta  | Sans-serif, muted, small                        | Dates, categories, secondary info |

### Principles
- The logo serif (Bodoni-style thick/thin) should appear only in brand/logo contexts.
- Body headlines use the editorial serif (Cormorant Garamond) — softer and more readable.
- The script accent can appear as decorative text in section headers or pull quotes.
- Never use heavy/black weights in body text — keep it light and open.

## Layout & Spacing

- **Whitespace is the primary design element** — sessions feel spacious and unhurried.
- **Max content width:** ~700px for text sections; hero and imagery can be full-bleed.
- **Section padding:** generous vertical padding (6–10rem between sections).
- **Split layouts:** dark/rich panel + imagery — used for hero sections.
- **Centered sections:** body copy centered on narrow columns with eyebrow + headline.
- **Grid:** primarily single-column or 50/50 split. Avoid busy multi-column grids.

## Components

### Hero
- Left panel: deep crimson or olive background, cream headline, ghost CTA button
- Right panel: full-bleed photography (art therapy session, colourful paint, workshop)
- Small eyebrow text above headline

### Buttons
- **Primary:** ghost/outlined — thin border, no fill, understated. Keep small.
- **Emphasis:** filled with crimson or olive, cream text — used sparingly.
- Slightly rounded corners (2–4px), not pill-shaped. Uppercase label, wide tracking.

### Tags / Pills
- Pill-shaped (`border-radius: 999px`)
- Blush peach fill or outlined in burnt sienna
- Used for service/specialty labels

### Botanical Illustrations
- Pencil-drawn line art of flowers, botanicals — used as section ornaments or background elements
- Monochrome (olive or ink), never colourful — provides refinement against the expressive photography

### Cards
- Rounded corners (8–12px)
- Fills: blush peach, olive dark (inverted), or steel-blue striped panel
- Subtle border, generous internal padding

### Press / Media Strip
- Horizontal row of logos, muted/desaturated
- "Featured In" eyebrow label left
- Hairline borders above and below

## Photography & Imagery Direction

- **Art therapy sessions:** people painting together, hands on canvas, creative collaboration
- **Tools & process:** paint brushes, colourful paint strokes on paper, pencils, chalk pastels
- **Coaching:** small group or 1-on-1 settings, warm and focused
- **Botanical line drawings:** elegant pencil illustrations as graphic accents (monochrome)
- **Colour temperature:** warm but not exclusively golden-hour — the colourful paint/process imagery
  brings energy and life. Balance warm neutral backgrounds with pops of expressive colour.
- **Never:** clinical, cold, stock-photo generic. Always: real, human, in-process.

---

## Mood & Voice

- Calm, grounded, creative
- Speaks directly and warmly — accessible without being casual
- Dutch cultural context: direct, no-nonsense warmth
- Headlines are inviting and clear, not overly poetic
- Avoids clinical jargon; prefers human, process-oriented language
- The brand holds both structure (elegant typography) and freedom (expressive colour and imagery)
